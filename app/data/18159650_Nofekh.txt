Nofekh () is a community settlement in central Israel. Located in the Shephelah, it falls under the jurisdiction of Hevel Modi'in Regional Council. In it had a population of . ==Etymology== Its name is taken from one of the 12 stones in the Hoshen, the sacred breastplate worn by a Jewish high priest, mentioned in Exodus 28:18. Nearby Shoham, Bareqet, Leshem and Ahlama (the former name of Beit Arif) were also named after the Hoshen stones. ==History== The village was established by immigrants from Morocco in 1949 on the land of the depopulated Palestinian village of Rantiya. ==References== Category:Community settlements Category:Populated places established in 1949 Category:Populated places in Central District (Israel) Category:1949 establishments in Israel Category:Moroccan-Jewish culture in Israel
